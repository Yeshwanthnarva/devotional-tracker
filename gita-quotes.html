Bhagavad Gita

भगवद्गीता — The Song of the Divine
The Bhagavad Gita contains 700 verses spoken by Lord Krishna to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra. Its wisdom is timeless — guiding us on duty, devotion, knowledge, and the path to liberation (Moksha). These are the most beloved verses chosen for daily reflection.
Chapter 2, Verse 47
कर्मण्येवाधिकारस्ते मा फलेषु कदाचन।
मा कर्मफलहेतुर्भूर्मा ते सङ्गोऽस्त्वकर्मणि॥
Karmaṇyevādhikāraste mā phaleṣu kadācana | Mā karmaphala-hetur bhūr mā te saṅgo'stvakarmaṇi ||
You have the right to perform your duties, but you are not entitled to the fruits of your actions. Never consider yourself the cause of results, and never be attached to inaction.
💡 Do your japa with full devotion, without worrying about results. The act of offering is itself the reward.
Chapter 2, Verse 23
नैनं छिन्दन्ति शस्त्राणि नैनं दहति पावकः।
न चैनं क्लेदयन्त्यापो न शोषयति मारुतः॥
Nainaṃ chindanti śastrāṇi nainaṃ dahati pāvakaḥ | Na cainaṃ kledayantyāpo na śoṣayati mārutaḥ ||
The soul cannot be cut by weapons, nor burned by fire. Water cannot wet it, nor can the wind dry it. The soul is eternal, indestructible, and immortal.
💡 You are not this body. You are the eternal Atman. Daily japa helps us remember our true nature.
Chapter 2, Verse 48
योगस्थः कुरु कर्माणि सङ्गं त्यक्त्वा धनञ्जय।
सिद्ध्यसिद्ध्योः समो भूत्वा समत्वं योग उच्यते॥
Yogasthaḥ kuru karmāṇi saṅgaṃ tyaktvā dhanañjaya | Siddhyasiddhyoḥ samo bhūtvā samatvaṃ yoga ucyate ||
Established in yoga, O Arjuna, perform your actions, abandoning attachment. Remain balanced in success and failure — this evenness of mind is called yoga.
💡 True yoga is not gymnastics — it is the balanced, steady mind that remains calm in all situations.
Chapter 4, Verse 7–8
यदा यदा हि धर्मस्य ग्लानिर्भवति भारत।
अभ्युत्थानमधर्मस्य तदात्मानं सृजाम्यहम्॥
परित्राणाय साधूनां विनाशाय च दुष्कृताम्।
धर्मसंस्थापनार्थाय सम्भवामि युगे युगे॥
Yadā yadā hi dharmasya glānir bhavati bhārata | Abhyutthānam adharmasya tadātmānaṃ sṛjāmyaham || Paritrāṇāya sādhūnāṃ vināśāya ca duṣkṛtām | Dharma-saṃsthāpanārthāya sambhavāmi yuge yuge ||
Whenever righteousness declines and unrighteousness rises, O Arjuna, I manifest Myself. For the protection of the good, the destruction of the wicked, and to firmly establish dharma — I am born age after age.
💡 The Divine Mother too descends in every age to protect Her devotees. Amma is that manifestation of Divine Love in our time.
Chapter 9, Verse 22
अनन्याश्चिन्तयन्तो मां ये जनाः पर्युपासते।
तेषां नित्याभियुक्तानां योगक्षेमं वहाम्यहम्॥
Ananyāścintayanto māṃ ye janāḥ paryupāsate | Teṣāṃ nityābhiyuktānāṃ yogakṣemaṃ vahāmyaham ||
For those who worship Me with devotion, meditating on My transcendental form — I carry what they lack and preserve what they have.
💡 When you chant Sri Matre Namaha with full devotion, the Divine Mother takes responsibility for your wellbeing. You only need to surrender.
Chapter 9, Verse 34
मन्मना भव मद्भक्तो मद्याजी मां नमस्कुरु।
मामेवैष्यसि युक्त्वैवमात्मानं मत्परायणः॥
Man-manā bhava mad-bhakto mad-yājī māṃ namaskuru | Māmevaiṣyasi yuktvaivaṃ ātmānaṃ mat-parāyaṇaḥ ||
Fix your mind on Me, be devoted to Me, worship Me, bow down to Me. So shall you come to Me. I promise you truly, for you are dear to Me.
💡 This is the entire path of Bhakti in four steps: think of God, be devoted, worship, and surrender. Daily japa fulfils all four.
Chapter 12, Verse 13–14
अद्वेष्टा सर्वभूतानां मैत्रः करुण एव च।
निर्ममो निरहङ्कारः समदुःखसुखः क्षमी॥
सन्तुष्टः सततं योगी यतात्मा दृढनिश्चयः।
मय्यर्पितमनोबुद्धिर्यो मद्भक्तः स मे प्रियः॥
Adveṣṭā sarvabhūtānāṃ maitraḥ karuṇa eva ca | Nirmamo nirahaṅkāraḥ sama-duḥkha-sukhaḥ kṣamī || Santuṣṭaḥ satataṃ yogī yatātmā dṛḍhaniścayaḥ | Mayyarpita-manobuddhir yo mad-bhaktaḥ sa me priyaḥ ||
One who bears ill will toward no being, who is friendly and compassionate, free from possessiveness and ego, equal in joy and sorrow, forgiving, always content, self-controlled, and with mind and intellect dedicated to Me — that devotee is very dear to Me.
💡 The qualities of the ideal devotee: compassion, equanimity, no ego. These are the qualities Amma embodies and teaches us.
Chapter 18, Verse 66
सर्वधर्मान्परित्यज्य मामेकं शरणं व्रज।
अहं त्वां सर्वपापेभ्यो मोक्षयिष्यामि मा शुचः॥
Sarva-dharmān parityajya mām ekaṃ śaraṇaṃ vraja | Ahaṃ tvāṃ sarva-pāpebhyo mokṣayiṣyāmi mā śucaḥ ||
Abandon all varieties of religion and just surrender unto Me alone. I shall deliver you from all sinful reactions. Do not fear.
💡 The final and highest teaching of the Gita — total surrender. Chanting "Sri Matre Namaha" is an act of complete surrender to the Divine Mother.
Chapter 6, Verse 5
उद्धरेदात्मनात्मानं नात्मानमवसादयेत्।
आत्मैव ह्यात्मनो बन्धुरात्मैव रिपुरात्मनः॥
Uddhared ātmanātmānaṃ nātmānam avasādayet | Ātmaiva hyātmano bandhur ātmaiva ripur ātmanaḥ ||
Elevate yourself through the power of your own mind, and do not degrade yourself. The mind can be your best friend or your worst enemy.
💡 You are your own greatest helper or greatest obstacle. Daily discipline in japa is how we train the mind to be our friend.
Chapter 2, Verse 20
न जायते म्रियते वा कदाचिन्नायं भूत्वा भविता वा न भूयः।
अजो नित्यः शाश्वतोऽयं पुराणो न हन्यते हन्यमाने शरीरे॥
Na jāyate mriyate vā kadācin nāyaṃ bhūtvā bhavitā vā na bhūyaḥ | Ajo nityaḥ śāśvato'yaṃ purāṇo na hanyate hanyamāne śarīre ||
The soul is never born, nor does it ever die. It has not come into being, does not come into being, and will not come into being. It is unborn, eternal, ever-existing, and primeval. It is not slain when the body is slain.
💡 Death is not the end — it is only a change of garment for the eternal soul. This knowledge removes fear and gives courage for spiritual practice.
🙏 "Sarvopaniṣado gāvo dogdhā gopālanandanaḥ | Pārtho vatsaḥ sudhīr bhoktā dugdhaṃ gītāmṛtaṃ mahat" 🙏
All Upanishads are like cows, Lord Krishna is the one who milks them, Arjuna is the calf, and the Bhagavad Gita's nectar is the milk. May its wisdom nourish your soul.
